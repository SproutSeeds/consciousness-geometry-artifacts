## Interpretation

The hybrid currently wins because it keeps speculative claims from moving
toward the evidential center while still allowing the source's developmental
sequence to be represented.

The radial evidence field is almost as strong and may become the default base
layer. The spiral is useful as an overlay but weak as a standalone evidence
structure. The torus model is promising for practice and relationship dynamics,
but too permissive for the full mixed source.

## Failure Condition

The hybrid fails if it can accept any claim without meaningful movement,
rejection, or tension.
